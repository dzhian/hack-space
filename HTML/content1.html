13th To 18th Century

By the period 1200-1300, women's clothing began to become truly ornate - a great deal of gold, silver, pearls and precious stones were used in the design of clothing and luxury was at its height. During this period, women would wear a tight-fitting jacket that extended below the hips, which had long, tight sleeves and a seam around the wrist, fastened with elaborate buttons. Of course, women could also opt for another drape-like gown garment, which would have been trimmed with elaborate embroidery and animal furs. And the big difference from the last hundred years was that the women of the 13th century were finally free from the grip of the headscarf and completely liberated their hair. So too did a number of exquisite hairstyles - they began to braid their hair, hanging down the side of their faces to their necks, and decorating it with pearls, gold and silver ornaments.

Since the Renaissance in the 15th century, women's quest for beauty was driven by the opening up of ideas to dress. They wore corsets on the top half of their bodies and women's skirts with wide hemlines on the bottom half. The corset was made of steel bars or whalebone. The baleen is cut into strips to support the garment; each corset has 40 or more baleen strips of varying length and thickness. Miraculously, the corset could cinch the waist from 19 inches to 14 inches.
